Our UX Approach in Line with the Design Thinking Process

01

Empathizing

We step into your customers' shoes to understand their needs and challenges. We may observe users in action and interview them about their experiences with similar products. This helps us grasp their perspective and identify the key problem to solve.

02

Defining the Problem

After understanding user needs and issues, we define the specific problem to tackle. We compile insights from the empathizing phase, leading to a clear, focused problem statement. This clarity is crucial for the next stage of generating solution ideas.
03

Ideating

We brainstorm, experiment, and try out different approaches to uncover an array of solutions. We also conduct co-creation workshops with project stakeholders, involving them in the design process. Then, we select one or two promising concepts to be transformed into prototypes and tested on real users.
04

Prototyping

We create scaled-down versions of the product, known as prototypes. They range from simple sketches to complex, interactive models. Our goal is to make the idea tangible and testable. We want to ensure it effectively addresses user problems before developing the full product.
05

Testing

We test the prototype with real users to gather their feedback. If needed, we revisit previous steps for further iterations. This helps us generate more ideas or explore alternative solutions until the solution fits users’ needs.

5 Steps to Create Your Product UI

Developing a Visual Style

We create a visual style guide that includes your brand's logos, typography, color palette, and layouts. This ensures a consistent look for your product or service.

Creating a Moodboard

Our designers make moodboards with images, colors, and typography to inspire and define the visual style of the interface. This also aids in developing the visual style guide.

Building a Design System

We combine all design elements into a system, defining how interface elements work and connect. This system acts as a toolkit for building the product interface.

Creating a Product Mockup

We build a mockup showing how the product screens will look, using your branding elements. This mockup, which can be low- or high-fidelity, and static or clickable, is used for user testing.

Usability Testing

We test the mockup with different user groups to evaluate its look and feel. Feedback from these tests helps us refine the design before final production.
