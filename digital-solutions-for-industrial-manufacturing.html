Why Go Digital?

To stay competitive, manufacturers are modernizing IT infrastructures and overhauling business processes to reduce costs, improve efficiency, and cut energy usage. Key drivers for digital transformation in manufacturing include:

Skills Shortage
2.4m A growing talent gap makes it harder to fill critical roles, pushing companies to rely on automation and digital tools.
Automation Gap
60% A large share of industrial tasks can be automated, creating opportunities for greater efficiency and productivity.
Equipment Downtime
$100k Even a short production stop can cost businesses heavily, making predictive maintenance and IoT monitoring essential.
Power Consumption
33% Manufacturing remains one of the largest energy-consuming industries, creating pressure to adopt smart, sustainable technologies

Digital Manufacturing Solutions

Case studies

Robotics

We develop embedded software, web and desktop applications, and mobile apps that support the management and smooth functioning of industrial robots, which work independently or in conjunction with humans. Our knowledge spans:

  • Warehouse and fulfillment center robots
  • Collaborative robots
  • Robot arms and grippers

Remote Sensing

HAND CHOICE installs sensors (temperature, motion, pressure, humidity, etc.), beacons, and video cameras on machinery and in industrial facilities to help our customers monitor equipment health and inventory location in real time. To this end, we turn to:

  • Cloud, fog, and edge computing
  • ML-driven data analysis
  • Computer vision
  • Front-end tools for data visualization
  • Device-to-browser video streaming

Predictive Maintenance

We use Machine Learning to uncover patterns in the data produced by connected equipment and disparate IT systems in a factory. HAND CHOICE further leverages these insights to design Predictive Maintenance solutions enabling manufacturers to:

  • Maximize equipment uptime
  • Avoid costly repairs
  • Boost production output
  • Reduce maintenance costs by up to 95%

Software for an AR Wearable Device for Field Workers

We turn to industrial Augmented Reality to help plant employees master complex equipment, receive visual instructions from technicians, and virtually modify products during the design stage. Our AR solution pool features:

  • Assembly accuracy
  • Holograms
  • Equipment manuals
  • Remote guidance

Green Manufacturing

In addition to the economic benefits, IoT in manufacturing can minimize negative impact on the environment. It can prevent material and energy waste, limit waste from products that don't meet quality standards, and make asset inspections more effective and safe. We use Internet of things, artificial intelligence, and UAVs, to create climate tech solutions for:

  • Energy efficiency 
  • More precise material usage
  • Remote drone-based inspections
